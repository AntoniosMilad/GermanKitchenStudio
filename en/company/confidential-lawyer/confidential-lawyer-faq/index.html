    Confidential lawyer FAQ

    Our FAQ contains answers to frequently asked questions on matters involving our Confidential lawyer.

    What tasks does the Ombudsman have?

    Employees are entitled to report circumstances that might suggest a violation of the law or internal regulations. The external entrusted attorney as Ombudsman accepts such information. They provide a legally protected and confidential environment outside the company. They advise the informant about their rights and the next steps. It is only with the informantâs consent that the Ombudsmanâs office forwards the information to their contact person in the company. The informant is not named. The Ombudsmanâs office may be included in the process as a person of trust. They are always available to the informant as a contact person.

    Employees, suppliers, sales partners and customers, among others, may contact the Ombudsmanâs office.

     

    What information does the Ombudsmanâs office accept?

    The Ombudsmanâs office is open to information about violations of the law and internal infringements. The primary objective is to clarify and prevent economic offences, balance sheet violations and asset misappropriation. But breaches of internal guidelines or violations of due diligence obligations under human rights might also warrant reporting.

     

    Can I be certain that the Ombudsmanâs office will only pass on information insofar as I allow it to do so?

    Yes. The informant alone decides as to which information they give to the Ombudsmanâs office and which information the Ombudsmanâs office should pass on to the company in a second step. It is only in cases of misuse, i.e. deliberately false information, that the Ombudsmanâs office is authorised to disclose information even against the will of the informant. The Ombudsmanâs office will explain this upon initial contact.

     

    Does it cost me anything to make use of the Ombudsman service?

    No, anyone can use the Ombudsmanâs service free of charge.

    Yes, informants can also contact the Ombudsmanâs office anonymously. This is the case even when you contact the Ombudsmanâs office for the first time. To the extent desired, the Ombudsmanâs office will then protect the anonymity of the informant against the company.

     

    What happens with my information?

    The information will be followed up with due regard for the law and internal regulations as well as the interests of all parties involved. To do this, the Ombudsmanâs office will forward the information to their point of contact in the company following prior examination.

     

    Am I free to contact the Ombudsmanâs office about the status of the proceedings?

    The informant may contact the Ombudsmanâs office at any time to obtain information on the current status. No later than after the conclusion of the process, the informant shall be informed of the result by the Ombudsmanâs office insofar as this is legally permissible.

    Yes. The Ombudsmanâs office is an autonomous and independent legal counsel that is not subject to any directives by the company with regard to the substantive treatment of cases. The Ombudsmanâs office decides whether and to what extent it may pass on a matter to the company after its own dutiful examination.

     

    How is first contact made?

    First contact can be made via the platform (contact link), in a telephone call, by email, by SMS, by post or in a face-to-face meeting.

     

    Can I continue to contact the previously responsible offices in the company?

    Yes. All employees continue to have their manager, the works council and all known points of reference at their disposal as their first point of contact.

     

    Am I protected as an informant?

    Yes. The informant is protected. Any retaliatory action directed against the informant will not be tolerated.

     

    How is misuse of the service (denunciation) prevented?

    In spite of the repeated scepticism, instances of denunciation are very rare with the service of an ombudsmanâs office. Nevertheless, the entrusted attorney shall explain to the informant at the beginning of the discussion that any abuse of the informant system will not be tolerated and that in the event of intentional or knowing abuse, the entrusted attorney is obliged to pass on the personal details of the informant to the company. Employees who deliberately misuse the informant system will always be subject to disciplinary consequences. 

     

    How is data protection and data security adhered to?

    What happens if I give information but it turns out to be wrong in the end?
